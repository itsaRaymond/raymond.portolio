Problem

At The Hague University, only 12% of Caribbean Dutch students graduate.

Overview

CarE (Caribbean Empowerment Network) is a community program supporting Dutch Caribbean students at The Hague University of Applied Sciences to enhance their academic success.

In this project, we aimed to make a digital tool to help Dutch Caribbean students do better in their studies. By conducting research we gained insights into their unique challenges. Subsequently, we designed an app to help their studies

Step one: Research

We wanted to get an insight on the following points:

  • What does Academic success mean to Dutch Caribbean students?
  • How important is Dutch proficiency?
  • What challenges do Dutch Caribbean face?

We used the following techniques:

  • desk research
  • surveys
  • love/break up letters
  • interviews

From our research we gained the following insights:

  • Academic success is not solely about academic performance but also encompasses one's overall life, efforts, and personal development.
  • Many students struggle with the Dutch language, especially speaking and colloquial language, posing a significant challenge for many Caribbean Dutch students.
  • Loneliness is a significant problem since many students come to the Netherlands alone and often struggle with the language.
  • There is insufficient assistance in handling practical matters (e.g., applying for student financing and housing).
  • As many students live on their own for the first time, they suddenly bear a lot of responsibility. This, combined with the demands of studying and adapting to a new culture, often leads to burnout and stress.
  • The weather is very different from what the students are accustomed to, and when combined with loneliness, it often results in homesickness.

Step two: Prototyping

To generate ideas we began by Brainstorming concepts:

We decided to concentrate our app on loneliness and weather because, from our research, they were the most common challenges. To generate ideas, we used various methods such as reverse brainstorming (thinking about the worst possible ideas to know what to avoid), brain writing, and card sorting.

Step three: Concept

After the brainstorm we had generated three different ideas:

  1. a communication platform
  2. a friend-making approach inspired by Tinder
  3. a planner that shares your progress with friends

After sketching and getting feedback, we combined the first two concepts to create CommuniCare. It's a social community app for Dutch Caribbean students to make friends, chat, join events, and play a weather game.
